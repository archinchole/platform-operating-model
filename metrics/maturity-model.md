# Engineering Maturity Model

## Overview

Engineering maturity is not defined by tools or team size.

It is defined by:

- consistency of outcomes  
- level of reuse  
- independence of teams  
- ability to scale without increasing effort linearly  

---

## Maturity Levels

### Level 1 — Delivery Driven

- Project-based execution  
- Outcomes depend on individual teams  
- Low reuse  
- High variability  

---

### Level 2 — Standardized Delivery

- Basic standardization across projects  
- Some shared practices  
- Partial reuse  
- Improved but inconsistent outcomes  

---

### Level 3 — Platform Enabled

- Shared services and components  
- Reduced duplication  
- Defined patterns  
- Moderate predictability  

---

### Level 4 — Capability Driven

- Repeatable solutions  
- Reuse by design  
- Teams operate with autonomy  
- High predictability  

---

### Level 5 — IP Led Organization

- Capabilities evolve into assets  
- Productized offerings  
- Engineering contributes to business scale  
- Strong differentiation  

---

## Key Indicators of Maturity

- Are outcomes consistent across similar projects?  
- Can teams operate independently?  
- Is reuse happening naturally?  
- Is delivery becoming more predictable over time?  

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Tool adoption without structural change  
- Platform built but not adopted  
- High dependency across teams  
- Repeated reinvention across projects  

---

## Summary

Maturity is not about doing more.

It is about:

> doing the same things, consistently, at scale.
